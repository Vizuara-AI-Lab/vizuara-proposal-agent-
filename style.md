# Vizuara Corporate Training Proposal — Style Guide

Extracted from 5 reference proposals: PACCAR India, Dell Technologies, Mahindra AI Division (LLM), Mahindra Group (AI), and Renesas Electronics.

---

## 1. Document Format

- **Typesetting**: LaTeX (compiled as PDF for delivery, authored in Overleaf)
- **Document class**: `article` with professional formatting
- **Branding**: Vizuara logo (top-left) and client logo (top-right) on the title page
- **Page numbers**: Bottom-center, Arabic numerals
- **Font**: Default LaTeX serif (Computer Modern or similar)

---

## 2. Two Proposal Archetypes

### Type A — Short-Form (Workshop / Bootcamp)
Used for: short engagements (1-12 days), focused training, in-person or Zoom workshops.
Examples: PACCAR (2-day, 10 hours), Dell (12-day program).

**Typical structure:**
1. Title page (program name, subtitle, "for CLIENT", "Conducted by Vizuara Technologies")
2. Training Overview box (Duration, Instructor, Audience, Format, Mode)
3. Program Objectives (bulleted)
4. Curriculum Structure (day-by-day, session-by-session)
5. Instructor Profile(s)
6. Deliverables
7. Pricing Options (table with tiers by participant count)
8. Why Choose Vizuara (bulleted)
9. Contact Us

### Type B — Long-Form (Semester / Multi-Month Program)
Used for: extended engagements, multi-course programs, self-paced + live hybrid.
Examples: Mahindra (both proposals), Renesas (5-month program).

**Typical structure:**
1. Title page ("Proposal for CLIENT", Agenda line, "Prepared by: Vizuara Technologies Private Limited")
2. Introduction (1 paragraph of formal prose)
3. Proposed Training Program / Overview of Courses
4. Delivery Modalities (Live + Self-Paced)
5. Course Schedule and Details (table)
6. Semester-Wise Repetition (if applicable)
7. Proposal / Pricing (table with tiers)
8. Deliverables
9. About Vizuara Technologies Private Limited
10. Contact Us
11. Appendix (detailed module breakdowns in tables)

## 4. Training Overview Box (Type A only)

A highlighted/bordered box immediately after the title page containing:
- **Total Duration**: X Hours (Y Hours per Day x Z Days)
- **Instructor**: Mr./Dr. [Name], [Title] at Vizuara Technologies
- **Audience**: [Description of target participants]
- **Format**: Interactive sessions with live demos and practical exercises
- **Mode**: In-person / Online (Zoom) / Hybrid

---

## 5. Sections — Content Patterns

### Introduction (Type B)
- Opens with: "Vizuara Technologies Private Limited is pleased to offer..." or "Vizuara Technologies Private Limited (Vizuara) proposes..."
- One paragraph outlining the program goal
- Followed by a bulleted list summarizing what the proposal covers
- Tone: formal, confident, client-oriented

**Example (Mahindra LLM):**
> "Vizuara Technologies Private Limited is pleased to offer a comprehensive set of training courses tailored specifically for the Mahindra AI-Division Team on Large Language Models (LLMs). Our goal is to empower your team with the necessary skills and knowledge to not only handle complex LLM-related challenges but also to eventually build in-house LLM solutions."

### Program Objectives (Type A)
- 3-5 bullet points
- Each starts with an action verb: "Help...", "Demonstrate...", "Show...", "Enable..."
- Focused on outcomes, not process

### Curriculum Structure
- Organized by Day or Phase
- Each day/phase has a bold heading: "Day 1 — {Topic} ({Hours})"
- Under each day: Session headings in bold, followed by bullet points
- Bullet points describe topics, not full sentences
- Mix of: concept introduction, live demo, hands-on exercise, group activity, discussion
- Always includes hands-on/practical elements per session
- Client-specific customization called out (e.g., "Building a simple FAQ chatbot for PACCAR")

### Instructor Profile(s)
- Name in bold, title at Vizuara
- 2-3 sentences about expertise
- Credentials: PhD institution, specialization, notable experience
- Key instructors:
  - **Dr. Raj Dandekar** — PhD, MIT / Purdue University, IIT Madras BTech, Co-founder, Vizuara AI
  - **Dr. Sreedath Panat** — PhD, MIT, Co-founder, Vizuara AI
  - **Pritam Kudale** — AI Expert / Lead AI Researcher, Vizuara Technologies
  - **Rishal C.** — Lead AI Trainer, Vizuara

### Deliverables
- Bulleted list, typically 4-5 items
- Common items:
  - X Hours of instructor-led practical sessions
  - Demo notebooks, code samples, and prompt templates
  - Post-training Q&A and resource pack
  - Certificate of Completion by Vizuara Technologies
  - Training Material Access (Slides + Templates + Code)
  - Dedicated company dashboard (for long-form programs)
  - Regular progress reports (for long-form programs)

### Pricing
- Presented as a LaTeX table
- Always includes "+ GST" for Indian clients
- USD for international clients (no GST note)
- Multiple tiers based on participant count or enrollment type
- Table columns: Description | Amount (INR/USD) — or — Type of Enrollment | Price | Offerings Included
- A footnote: "*Prices include instructor time, materials, and logistics. GST applicable as per norms."

**Pricing table examples:**

Short-form (PACCAR):
| Description | Amount (INR) |
|---|---|
| Two-Day (10 Hours) Corporate Training | Rs 3,25,000 |
| Training Material Access | Included |
| **Total Investment (All Inclusive)** | **Rs 3,25,000 + GST** |

Long-form (Mahindra):
| Type of Enrollment | Price | Offerings Included |
|---|---|---|
| For Individual Candidates (set of 3 courses) | Rs. 40,000 + GST | 3 hours/week live sessions, Self-paced access, ... |
| For [Client] (150 licenses, bulk rate) | Rs. 15,00,000 + GST | 1-year access, Dedicated dashboard, ... |

### Why Choose Vizuara / About Vizuara

- "Experts in enterprise upskilling and applied AI education."
- "Focused on making AI approachable and immediately useful."
- "Hands-on, tool-based delivery with interactive demos."
- "Customized content tailored for {CLIENT}'s business context."

**Long version (Type B — "About Vizuara Technologies Private Limited"):**
> "Vizuara is founded by three graduates from **IIT Madras** and PhDs from **MIT (USA)** and **Purdue (USA)**. The founders are experts in AI and have worked in prestigious AI companies. ## 6. Appendix (Type B only)

- Detailed module-by-module breakdown in LaTeX tables
- Table columns: Module No. | Unit No. | Topics | Ref. | Hours
- Module titles highlighted with colored row headers
- Each module has 2-5 units
- References column uses numbers (1,2,3...) pointing to textbooks/resources

## 7. Voice and Tone

- **Formality**: Professional but approachable. Not academic, not casual.
- **Person**: Third person for Vizuara ("Vizuara offers..."), second person for client ("your team").
- **Confidence level**: Assertive without being pushy. "Our goal is to empower..." not "We hope to..."
- **Action-oriented**: Emphasizes what participants will DO, BUILD, CREATE.
- **Key recurring phrases**:
  - "empower your team"
  - "hands-on"
  - "practical exercises"
  - "real-world"
  - "interactive sessions"
  - "live demos"
  - "customized/tailored for {CLIENT}"
  - "AI capacity building"
  - "in-house AI solutions"
  - "from 'knowing' AI to 'using' AI effectively"
- **Avoids**: Jargon-heavy language when describing program to non-technical audiences. Technical depth is reserved for curriculum bullet points.

### Email-Specific Tone
- **Short and concise** — emails should be scannable in under 20 seconds. The proposal does the heavy lifting; the email just opens the door.
- **Not salesy** — avoid marketing language, bullet-point highlights, or "selling" the program in the email. State what's attached. That's it.
- **Not desperate** — no "happy to schedule a call", "looking forward to hearing from you", or eager follow-up language. A simple "Do let us know if you have any questions" is sufficient.
- **Direct** — get to the point quickly. One short opening line, one short paragraph summarizing what's attached, one brief closing line.
- **Target length**: 60-100 words max (excluding signature).

## 8. Formatting Conventions

- **Section numbering**: Arabic (1, 2, 3...) with subsections (2.1, 2.2...)
- **Bold**: Used for session titles, key terms, instructor names, important notes
- **Italics**: Used for course names, emphasis, dates
- **Bullet style**: Filled circles (LaTeX default `\item`)
- **Tables**: Bordered with header row, clean professional look
- **Horizontal rules**: Used sparingly to separate title page from content
- **No emojis** anywhere in the document

---

## 9. Client Customization Points

Every proposal is customized at these specific touchpoints:
1. **Title page**: Client name and logo
2. **Training Overview**: Audience description mentions client context
3. **Curriculum**: At least 1-2 sessions mention client-specific applications (e.g., "PACCAR Document Assistant", "Mahindra-specific problems")
4. **Pricing**: Tailored to client's team size and budget
5. **Why Vizuara bullet**: "Customized content tailored for {CLIENT}'s business context"
6. **Contact/footer**: "Prepared for: {CLIENT}"

---

## 10. Content Depth by Audience Level

| Audience | Curriculum Style | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Business teams, non-technical | Intuition-first, no math, demos and tools | PACCAR |
| Engineers with some AI background | Concepts + code, frameworks, hands-on projects | Dell Phase I-III |
| AI/ML practitioners | Architecture details, from-scratch implementations, production deployment | Dell Phase IV, Renesas |
| AI division / research teams | Deep technical content, papers, advanced architectures | Mahindra LLM, Renesas Part 4-5 |

---

## 11. Recurring Curriculum Building Blocks

These are common session types that appear across multiple proposals and can be mixed and matched:

- **Generative AI Intro**: What is AI, GenAI, demos with ChatGPT/DALL-E
- **LLM Fundamentals**: How language models work, tokenization, transformers
- **Prompt Engineering**: Basic and advanced prompting techniques
- **RAG Systems**: Retrieval-Augmented Generation, vector search, LangChain
- **AI Agents**: ReAct, LangGraph, multi-agent systems, tool use
- **Building LLMs from Scratch**: Tokenization, training, architecture
- **Fine-tuning**: LoRA, QLoRA, SFT, DPO
- **Production/Deployment**: Kubernetes, vLLM, Triton, CI/CD
- **Chatbot Building**: FAQ bots, document assistants
- **Ethics and Responsible AI**: Bias, guardrails, safety
- **Hands-on Capstone Projects**: Client-specific problem-solving
